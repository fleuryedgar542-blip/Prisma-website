Why standard planners do not work for a neurodivergent brain

Written by Fleur Yedgar · March 2026 · 5 min read

← Back to blog

You tried a new planner again. Or downloaded an app. Or tested a system someone recommended. A few days later it is already sitting unused.

Not because you are lazy, and not because you do not care. More often, it is because standard planners were built for a neurotypical brain, and yours works differently.

What standard planners get wrong

Most planners assume your energy is basically the same every day. They assume you can start the morning with your hardest task, move through meetings and admin in a straight line, and still have enough left for evening life.

With ADHD or autism, energy can shift hour by hour. What felt easy yesterday can feel impossible today. Not because you lack discipline, but because your nervous system is not static.

The four biggest problems

  • They start with tasks instead of energy.
  • They punish skipped days through streaks or pressure.
  • They expect easy long-term planning even when the present already feels full.
  • They ignore sensory and emotional load.

What an ADHD day planner actually needs

  • A starting point in energy, not productivity
  • Room for what today is really like
  • No shame mechanics, no streaks, no punishment
  • A short flow that still feels usable on hard days
  • Space for needs as well as tasks

That is the thinking behind PRISMA Day. It is not designed to force an ideal day. It helps you start from the day you actually have.
