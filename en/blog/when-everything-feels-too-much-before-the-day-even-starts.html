Overwhelm

When everything feels too much before the day even starts

Written by Fleur Yedgar · March 2026 · 8 min read

← Back to blog

Some mornings it is there straight away. Not one clear thought. More a feeling that the day is already too full before it has even started. You have not done anything yet, but your system already feels tight. As if too much is coming at you all at once while you are still in bed.

That can be hard to explain. Especially when the day does not even look that busy on paper. Then it is easy to assume you are overreacting. But most of the time the problem is not the number of appointments alone. It is how heavy the whole day feels to a system that already has very little room.

The day starts before the first appointment

For many people, the day starts when they begin doing things. For an overstimulated or low-energy system, it starts much earlier. It starts when you wake up. When you feel your body. When you remember there are expectations waiting. When you realise you will need to switch, respond, speak, decide and hold yourself together.

If you are already tired, slept badly or used up more than you had yesterday, all of that can feel immediately close. So it makes sense that everything already feels too much before you have brushed your teeth. Your nervous system counts just as much as your calendar.

Why ordinary planning does not help then

On mornings like this, standard planning often does very little. A list, a schedule or a good productivity tip can even make it worse. Not because you do not want help. But because those things assume a level of internal steadiness that is not there. They assume you have enough distance to make clean decisions.

When everything feels too much, your brain usually wants less in view. Fewer options. Less demand. Less scanning. And that is exactly where a lot of planning goes wrong. It does not shrink the day. It spreads the whole thing out in front of you.

That is also why some people look at their planner and shut down. The planner itself may be fine. The state they are looking at it from is not being considered.

Sometimes it is simply too much

There are days when you might actually be able to do things once you get moving. But the intake is too high. Too many impressions. Too many possible tasks. Too much internal noise. Then even something small can feel like the opening move in a much bigger chain. Replying to one message does not feel like one action. It feels like more contact, more reactions and more demand entering the room.

That is why bad days can be so invisible from the outside. Not much has happened yet. But inside, almost everything already feels like the start of more. And that is often why nothing happens. Not because you do not care, but because your system is protecting itself.

A small shift helps more than pushing harder

What often helps more is a smaller question. What does my system not need to keep carrying right now? That question is softer. You do not have to understand the whole day yet. You only have to notice where the pressure is sitting.

Sometimes it is the pressure of too many open loops. Sometimes it is the feeling that everything must happen today. Sometimes it is just the atmosphere of rush. If any part of that can come down, the day may not become easy, but it often becomes less solid and less threatening.

The day may need to get smaller first

At moments like this, it helps more to shrink the day than to organise it well. Not everything needs to be visible at once. Not everything needs to be decided now. The real question is not what the best plan is. The question is what your system can carry without locking up even more.

That is why I reach for PRISMA Day instead of a normal planner on days like this. To get back to what is actually realistic. How much energy is there? What is needed first? What kind of work style fits today? That makes the entry point smaller.

And if what you are feeling is more sensory than mental, PRISMA Signal can help too. Especially on the kind of mornings where you cannot even fully explain what is wrong, but you can tell your system is already too full.

Smaller often helps more

What many people need on days like this is less intake. Fewer open tabs. Fewer floating expectations. Less pretending the day is normal when the body is already telling a different story.

That does not mean giving up. It means being honest earlier. And here, honesty is often more practical than optimism. Because once you admit the day already feels heavy, you can stop treating yourself like the only problem is effort.

Often that changes something small. Not huge. Not dramatic. But enough. A little more air appears. One possible way in returns. And on hard days that is already a lot.
