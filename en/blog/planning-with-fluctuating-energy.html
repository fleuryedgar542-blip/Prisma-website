Planning with fluctuating energy

Written by Fleur Yedgar · March 2026 · 6 min read

← Back to blog

Many planners pretend energy is stable. As if Monday, Wednesday and Friday all give you the same amount of focus, tolerance and mental room. A lot of neurodivergent people know that is simply not true.

Capacity moves. Sleep, stress, noise, emotional load, social contact, hormones, recovery and uncertainty all change what a day can hold. If you keep planning as though every day is equal, you end up overshooting and then blaming yourself for the mismatch.

The issue is not a lack of discipline

Usually the real issue is that your planning system is not designed for a changing nervous system. It expects consistency first and adaptation later. Neurodivergent planning often needs the opposite.

What helps instead

  • Plan in ranges, not perfect schedules.
  • Give yourself a minimum version of the day or week.
  • Treat rest as part of the plan, not proof of failure.
  • Review energy, not just output.

A range might look like this: on a high-energy day, task A, B and C may fit. On a medium day, maybe only A and B. On a low day, even A may need to become prep or pause. That way the plan stays usable when your capacity shifts.
