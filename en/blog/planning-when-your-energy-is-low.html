Planning when your energy is low

Written by Fleur Yedgar · March 2026 · 6 min read

← Back to blog

Some seasons are not a short dip. Your system is simply carrying less energy for longer. Burnout, illness, sensory overload, grief or sustained stress can all change what planning needs to look like.

That does not make planning irrelevant. It often means planning is not the real problem. The real problem is that you have lost some sense of control, while most planning advice still assumes full access to energy and focus.

What low energy does to planning

  • Everything feels bigger than it is.
  • Decisions cost more effort.
  • Short lists can still feel overwhelming.
  • You compare yourself to an older capacity level that is not available right now.

That comparison often hurts the most. You plan for the version of yourself you remember, then feel like you failed when your current body cannot follow.

A gentler way to regain some control

Try separating what is truly necessary, what is optional if energy appears and what explicitly does not have to happen today. That last category matters. It turns vague pressure into boundaries your brain can actually use.

Calm direction still counts

Planning that includes recovery, pacing and letting things go is still planning. It is not a lesser version. Sometimes it is simply the only kind that respects what your system can carry right now.
