ADHD day planner

An ADHD day planner that starts with energy.

Many planners jump straight to tasks, priorities and discipline. But when your brain is already tired, overstimulated or scattered, the real problem usually is not your to-do list. It is your available capacity.

Why daily planning can feel hard with ADHD

ADHD days are rarely neutral. Energy can already be low in the morning. Focus can arrive late, disappear suddenly or split into ten directions. Sensory input can take up more room than expected. If a planner ignores that reality, it can start to feel like one more thing you are failing at.

What standard planners often miss

  • They start with tasks, not energy.
  • They assume the same capacity every day.
  • They leave little room for support needs or recovery.
  • They easily turn skipped days into shame.

How PRISMA Day works differently

PRISMA Day is a calm ADHD day planner. It helps you check in with energy, needs and work style before deciding what is realistic. That changes the tone of the whole day. Instead of forcing yourself into a plan, you shape a plan around the day you actually have.

Why that matters

  • Planning feels more honest and less punishing.
  • You get a clearer sense of what today can hold.
  • Low-capacity days stay usable instead of becoming lost days.
  • You are more likely to keep using the tool because it respects your nervous system.

Try the calmer option.

PRISMA Day runs in your browser, needs no account and is designed to stay useful even when your energy is low.
