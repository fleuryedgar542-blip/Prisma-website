Planner for autism

A planner that supports calm and predictability.

Many planners are visually busy, abstract or full of moving parts. For a lot of autistic people that creates more friction, not more clarity. A useful planner reduces noise instead of adding it.

What often goes wrong with ordinary planners

Standard planners can assume too much flexibility, too much tolerance for uncertainty, or too little awareness of sensory load. When you need clear structure, gentle pacing and lower sensory friction, that can make planning feel harder than the day itself.

What an autism-friendly planner often needs

  • Clear steps without unnecessary clutter
  • Room for recovery, preparation and sensory needs
  • Realistic structure built around actual capacity
  • Supportive tools that do not overwhelm the user

How PRISMA fits that need

PRISMA Day helps you shape a day from the inside out. PRISMA Signal helps you notice sensory overload earlier. PRISMA Week helps you spot patterns over time. Together they create a calmer planning system that respects energy and nervous-system reality.
